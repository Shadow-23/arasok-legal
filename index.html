Arasok Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 13, 2026

Arasok is a workout-tracking app developed by PDSJ Labs. This policy explains what information the app collects, how it is used, and how it is stored.

1. Data we collect on your device

All of your training data stays on your device unless you choose to export it. This includes:

  • Workouts, exercises, sets, reps, weights, and session notes you log
  • Optional profile information you enter (display name, age, height, weight, training goals, injury history, notes)
  • Body measurements you record
  • A profile photo, if you choose to add one from your device's photo library

This data is stored in a local database on your phone. It is not transmitted to PDSJ Labs or to any third party.

2. Optional Google Sign-In

Signing in with Google is optional. If you choose to sign in, the app receives the following from Google:

  • Your email address
  • Your display name
  • Your Google profile photo URL
  • A stable Google account identifier

This information is stored locally on your device for the purpose of identifying your account. It is not sent to PDSJ Labs servers. Sign-in is currently used to prepare for an optional cloud-sync feature; when that feature ships, this policy will be updated to describe what is synced and how.

3. Information shared with third parties

PDSJ Labs does not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties. Arasok does not include advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, or crash-reporting SDKs.

4. Network access

The app uses the internet only for:

  • Authenticating you when you tap "Sign in with Google" (handled by Google's Credential Manager)
  • Loading your Google profile photo, if you are signed in

5. Children

Arasok is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

6. Data retention and deletion

Because your data is stored locally on your device, you can delete it at any time by uninstalling Arasok or clearing the app's data in your device settings. Signing out within the app removes the cached Google identity from the device.

7. Changes to this policy

If material changes are made to this policy, the "Last updated" date above will be revised and an in-app notice will surface on the next app launch.
